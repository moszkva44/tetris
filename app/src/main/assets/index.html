*Naive gravity: This is the standard tetris physics you probably know well. It is naive because after rows being cleared some pieces may remain floating so the gravity works only until the given shape is placed at the first time. *Cascade gravity: The gravity affects pieces even after a shape being placed. If rows get cleared and therefore some pieces miss their underpinning, they will fall until they get a stable position again either on the ground or on another piece.
